# Channel Capacity Notes

Channel capacity is the maximum reliable information rate under a channel model.

Key ideas:

- noisy channels can still support reliable communication;
- rates below capacity can be made reliable in principle with suitable coding;
- rates above capacity cannot be made reliable under the model;
- assumptions about bandwidth, noise, input distribution, and channel behavior matter;
- practical code construction is a separate engineering problem.

Capacity is a boundary, not a guarantee of meaning.
