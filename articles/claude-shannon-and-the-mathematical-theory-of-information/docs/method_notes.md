# Method Notes

This folder treats Shannon as the architect of mathematical information theory.

The core method is:

1. Read Shannon's 1948 paper as communication theory, not a theory of all meaning.
2. Identify the communication system: source, transmitter, channel, noise, receiver, destination.
3. Define information as uncertainty reduction.
4. Compute entropy for simple sources.
5. Distinguish compression from error correction.
6. Analyze channel capacity and noise assumptions.
7. Study cryptography and secrecy as information-flow problems.
8. Connect information theory to algorithms, AI systems, data governance, and semantic caution.

The goal is to use information theory precisely while respecting its boundaries.
