# Measurement Justice Notes

Fairness cannot be stronger than the measurement system underneath it.

Review:

- whether labels reflect the intended construct;
- whether proxies substitute for harder realities such as merit, need, risk, quality, value, or safety;
- whether measurement error differs across groups;
- whether missing data is uneven or meaningful;
- whether categories misrepresent people;
- whether affected people can correct records;
- whether administrative convenience has become a hidden objective.

A fairness metric over a flawed label may only compare patterns inside an unjust measurement system.
