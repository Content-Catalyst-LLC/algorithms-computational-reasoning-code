# Governance Review

Contestability and algorithmic due-process governance should include:

- decision-stakes classification;
- model-use disclosure;
- reason-code design;
- evidence access and data-correction procedures;
- appeal channels and accessibility review;
- human reviewer authority;
- correction propagation;
- remediation procedures;
- audit trails and version logs;
- appeal rate, reversal rate, resolution time, and recurrence monitoring;
- subgroup and accessibility analysis.

A decision process should not be described as contestable unless people can actually use it to obtain review and repair.
