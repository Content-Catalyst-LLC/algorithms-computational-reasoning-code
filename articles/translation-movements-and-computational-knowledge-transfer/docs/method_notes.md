# Method Notes

This folder treats translation movements as computational knowledge infrastructure.

The core method is:

1. Identify the knowledge object: theorem, algorithm, table, diagram, instrument, recipe, or device.
2. Identify the language path: Greek, Syriac, Arabic, Persian, Sanskrit, Hebrew, Latin, or another route.
3. List procedural components: terms, steps, examples, diagrams, tables, units, and assumptions.
4. Check vocabulary mapping: which technical terms had to be borrowed, coined, glossed, or adapted?
5. Check diagrams and tables for continuity, labels, values, units, and structure.
6. Identify commentary, correction, revision, or teaching practices.
7. Ask what changed in the receiving environment: instruments, institutions, pedagogy, or questions.
8. Avoid single-origin narratives by mapping networks and relays.

The goal is to treat translation as the transfer of usable procedure, not just the movement of words.
