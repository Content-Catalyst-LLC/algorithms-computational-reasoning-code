# Method Notes

This folder treats programming-language history as a layered history of computational expression.

The core method is:

1. Identify the language's target users and problems.
2. Study its model of computation and control.
3. Analyze data representation and memory assumptions.
4. Examine compiler, interpreter, runtime, and tooling.
5. Trace libraries, standards, packages, and documentation.
6. Compare safety, error, and maintainability mechanisms.
7. Study adoption by institutions and communities.
8. Apply historical lessons to AI-generated code.

The goal is to avoid shallow language rankings and instead understand languages as expressive, technical, institutional, and governed artifacts.
