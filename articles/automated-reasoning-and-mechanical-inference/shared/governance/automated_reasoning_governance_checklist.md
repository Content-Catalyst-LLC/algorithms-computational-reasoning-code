# Automated Reasoning Governance Checklist

- State the formal system, logic, solver, or rule engine used.
- Identify premises, facts, assumptions, constraints, and axioms.
- Identify inference rules and decision procedures.
- Define the target claim being proved, checked, satisfied, or refuted.
- Preserve proof objects, models, assignments, traces, or counterexamples.
- Distinguish proven, checked, generated, inferred, suggested, unknown, and out-of-scope claims.
- Review whether the formalization represents the real problem adequately.
- Preserve source provenance and rule-version history.
- Provide human review for high-stakes, ambiguous, or contested conclusions.
- Avoid treating solver output as broader than its formal scope.
