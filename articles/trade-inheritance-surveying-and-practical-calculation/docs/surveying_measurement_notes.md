# Surveying and Measurement Notes

Surveying turns spatial situations into quantities.

Common procedure:

1. represent land or structure as a geometric form;
2. decompose irregular forms into simpler shapes;
3. measure relevant dimensions;
4. compute area, volume, or boundary;
5. combine parts;
6. check units and assumptions;
7. preserve a written or diagrammatic record.

Surveying shows how geometric procedure becomes institutional knowledge.
