# Auditability and Trust Notes

Practical calculation often requires trust.

A result is more trustworthy when the procedure is:

- recorded;
- checkable;
- repeatable;
- interpretable;
- tied to a known rule;
- open to correction;
- linked to units and context;
- reviewable by another person.

Written reckoning, ledgers, diagrams, worked examples, and records are early audit trails.
