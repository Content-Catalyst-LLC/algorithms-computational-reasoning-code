# Method Notes

This folder treats practical calculation as institutional algorithmic reasoning.

The core method is:

1. Identify the practical domain: trade, inheritance, surveying, administration, or education.
2. Extract quantities, units, shares, people, places, rates, or boundaries.
3. Classify the problem type and select the relevant rule.
4. Normalize units, shares, proportions, or geometric forms.
5. Perform the calculation step by step.
6. Check totals, remainders, units, or geometric consistency.
7. Interpret the answer in the original institutional setting.
8. Ask who can inspect, contest, or rely on the result.

The goal is to keep the social context visible while still extracting the procedure.
