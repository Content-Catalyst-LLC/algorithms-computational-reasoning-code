# Algorithmic Risk Management and AI Governance

This companion folder supports the Sustainable Catalyst article **Algorithmic Risk Management and AI Governance**.

It provides reproducible, teaching-oriented workflows for algorithmic risk management, AI governance, risk mapping, impact assessment, lifecycle controls, risk registers, control registers, ownership, monitoring, evaluation, incident response, vendor governance, contestability, documentation, auditability, remediation, and responsible institutional oversight.

## Article sequence

- Previous: `responsible-automation-and-decision-delegation`
- Current: `algorithmic-risk-management-and-ai-governance`
- Next: `documentation-model-cards-and-datasheets-for-algorithms`

## What this folder demonstrates

- how algorithmic risks can be named, scored, assigned, monitored, and remediated;
- how governance readiness can combine ownership, documentation, monitoring, contestability, remediation, and stop authority;
- how residual risk depends on inherent risk and control effectiveness;
- how risk registers and control registers connect technical systems to institutional responsibility;
- how governance artifacts, calculators, tests, SQL queries, Canvas cards, and multilingual examples can support accountable lifecycle review.

## Repository structure

- `python/` — reference algorithmic risk and AI governance audit workflow and tests
- `r/` — diagnostic summaries and figures
- `sql/` — schema and governance queries for risks, controls, residual risk, and escalation
- `calculators/` — self-contained inherent-risk, governance-readiness, residual-risk, and control-effectiveness calculators
- `canvas/` — Canvas-ready cards, schemas, and exports
- `docs/` — method notes, data dictionary, risk-register notes, governance review, and use boundaries
- language folders — compact examples across Python, R, Julia, SQL, Haskell, Rust, Go, C, C++, Fortran, Java, TypeScript, Prolog, and Racket

All datasets and examples are synthetic and educational. They are not legal, compliance, audit certification, civil-rights, medical, credit, employment, public-benefits, or operational advice.
