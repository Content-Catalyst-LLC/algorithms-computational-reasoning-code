# Data Dictionary

## sequenced_action_themes.csv

- `theme_id`: interpretive theme in sequenced mechanical action.
- `sequence_structure`: importance of ordered stages, cycles, and state transitions.
- `timing_control`: importance of water timing, cam timing, flow rate, interval marking, or delayed release.
- `mechanical_embodiment`: importance of parts such as cams, gears, levers, floats, vessels, weights, and linkages.
- `conditional_action`: importance of triggers, thresholds, latches, and state-dependent action.
- `repeatability`: importance of cycles, reset, iteration, and repeated useful work.
- `documentation_quality`: importance of diagrams, construction notes, operational explanations, and reproducibility.
- `historical_significance`: importance for the history of automata, Islamic-world engineering, and mechanical reasoning.
- `ethical_caution`: importance of careful non-anachronistic interpretation.
- `modern_resonance`: degree to which the theme clarifies automation, implementation, and embodied computation.

## sequenced_action_map.csv

- `sequenced_action_score`: average of the theme dimensions.
- `interpretive_status`: core, major, or supporting sequenced mechanical action thread.

## interpretation_cautions.csv

- `caution`: interpretive risk to avoid.
- `meaning`: explanation of the caution.
