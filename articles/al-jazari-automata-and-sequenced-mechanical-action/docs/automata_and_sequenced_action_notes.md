# Automata and Sequenced Action Notes

Automata produce visible behavior through internal arrangement.

Interpretive analogies:

- initial condition: setup or starting state;
- trigger: threshold, latch, cam contact, or release;
- output: visible motion, sound, pouring, or display;
- reset: return to cycle-ready condition;
- sequence: ordered chain of actions;
- hidden state: internal level, position, or stored potential.

Use these analogies carefully. They clarify structure but should not erase historical difference.
