# Method Notes

This folder treats al-Khwārizmī as a central figure in procedural history rather than a single-origin myth.

The core method is:

1. Identify the procedural domain: arithmetic, algebra, astronomy, geography, or practical calculation.
2. Describe the representation: numerals, words, tables, diagrams, or quantities.
3. Recover the operations: restore, balance, multiply, divide, carry, borrow, interpolate, classify.
4. Determine the problem class and stopping condition.
5. Trace transmission through Arabic, Latin, educational, and practical contexts.
6. Separate etymological importance from total-origin claims.
7. Connect the procedure to modern computational concepts without projecting modern code backward.
8. Place al-Khwārizmī in a multi-civilizational history of mathematical procedure.

The examples are dependency-light and educational.
