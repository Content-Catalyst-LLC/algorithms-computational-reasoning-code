# Procedural Imagination Notes

The procedural imagination is the ability to see a problem as something that can be transformed through steps.

Core questions:

- What is the input?
- What is the representation?
- What type of problem is it?
- Which operations are valid?
- What relation must remain invariant?
- What is the stopping condition?
- How is the result checked?
- Can the method be taught and reused?

Al-Khwārizmī's legacy matters because arithmetic, algebra, tables, and translation all show procedure becoming teachable and transmissible.
