# Origin Story Cautions

Avoid these claims:

- Al-Khwārizmī invented all algorithms.
- Algebra began from nothing in one book.
- Medieval procedures were computer programs.
- Islamic-world mathematics merely preserved earlier work.
- Etymology explains the entire history.
- One heroic figure is enough to explain procedural transmission.

Prefer these claims:

- Al-Khwārizmī is central to the naming and transmission history of algorithmic reasoning.
- His works helped systematize and teach major traditions of arithmetic and algebra.
- The Islamic world translated, extended, organized, and transmitted procedural mathematics.
- Algorithms have multiple lineages, including pre-computer procedures.
