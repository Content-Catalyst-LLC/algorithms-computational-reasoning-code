# Data Dictionary

## mechanical_procedure_themes.csv

- `theme_id`: interpretive theme in mechanical procedure.
- `mechanical_structure`: importance of physical components such as chambers, channels, valves, floats, and vessels.
- `procedural_sequence`: degree to which the theme involves ordered steps or timed transitions.
- `conditional_control`: importance of threshold, gating, switching, or state-dependent behavior.
- `hidden_state`: importance of concealed chambers, pressure, internal levels, or invisible control.
- `feedback_potential`: degree to which state or output can affect later behavior.
- `historical_significance`: importance for the history of mechanical reasoning and Islamic-world engineering.
- `ethical_caution`: importance of careful framing and non-anachronistic interpretation.
- `modern_resonance`: degree to which the theme clarifies modern automation and embodied computation.

## mechanical_procedure_map.csv

- `mechanical_score`: average of the theme dimensions.
- `interpretive_status`: core, major, or supporting mechanical procedure thread.

## interpretation_cautions.csv

- `caution`: interpretive risk to avoid.
- `meaning`: explanation of the caution.
