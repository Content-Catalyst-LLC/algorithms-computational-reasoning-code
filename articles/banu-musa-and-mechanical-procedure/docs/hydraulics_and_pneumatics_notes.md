# Hydraulics and Pneumatics Notes

Hydraulics and pneumatics are central to many Banū Mūsā devices.

Key ideas:

- liquid level can act as state;
- pressure difference can drive transition;
- sealed chambers can store hidden condition;
- narrow channels can delay action;
- siphons can trigger only after a threshold;
- outlets display visible output;
- leakage, friction, and construction accuracy affect behavior.

The point is not that these devices are digital computers. The point is that they embody procedure through physical arrangement.
