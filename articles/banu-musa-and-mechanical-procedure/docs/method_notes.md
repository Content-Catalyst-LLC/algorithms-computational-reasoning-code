# Method Notes

This folder treats Banū Mūsā mechanical devices as embodied procedure.

The core method is:

1. Identify the device type: vessel, fountain, lamp, control mechanism, or display device.
2. List parts: chamber, channel, valve, float, siphon, outlet, reservoir, weight, or seal.
3. Identify inputs: liquid, air, pressure, user action, flame, initial filling, or release.
4. Trace state changes: level, pressure, valve position, flow direction, or timing.
5. Find thresholds: when a float rises, siphon begins, valve opens, or pressure changes.
6. Identify output: pouring, stopping, alternating, measuring, regulating, or displaying.
7. Ask how the diagram and text support reproduction, teaching, or correction.
8. Compare with modern automation only after preserving historical difference.

The goal is to treat mechanical devices as procedures made from matter, geometry, and motion.
