# The Banū Mūsā and Mechanical Procedure

This companion folder supports the Sustainable Catalyst article **The Banū Mūsā and Mechanical Procedure**.

It provides reproducible, teaching-oriented workflows for mapping Banū Mūsā mechanical devices as embodied procedure: hydraulic material logic, pneumatic pressure control, valves, floats, thresholds, siphons, hidden chambers, timing, self-regulation, trick vessels, alternating fountains, diagrams, reproducibility, and material constraint.

## What this folder demonstrates

- how a mechanical device can embody step-by-step procedure;
- how fluid level, pressure, valves, floats, and chambers can act as state variables and conditional controls;
- how mechanical timing can be created through flow rate, volume, thresholds, and hidden reservoirs;
- how hydraulic and pneumatic systems can encode rule-governed behavior without modern software;
- how diagrams and instructions make mechanical procedure reproducible;
- how mechanical devices should be framed carefully as automata and embodied procedure, not modern robots, computers, or AI;
- how material constraints such as leakage, friction, pressure, seals, construction accuracy, and implementation fidelity matter.

## Repository structure

- `python/` — reference mechanical-procedure map workflow and tests
- `r/` — mechanical-procedure diagnostics and figures
- `sql/` — schema and queries for mechanical themes, scores, and interpretation cautions
- `calculators/` — self-contained threshold, flow-time, valve-gate, reservoir-volume, pressure-difference, state-transition, and mechanical-score calculators
- `canvas/` — Canvas-ready cards, schemas, and exports
- `docs/` — method notes, data dictionary, hydraulic/pneumatic notes, valves/floats notes, diagrams/reproducibility notes, and use boundaries
- language folders — compact examples across Python, R, Julia, SQL, Haskell, Rust, Go, C, C++, Fortran, Java, TypeScript, Prolog, and Racket

All datasets and examples are synthetic and interpretive. They are educational scaffolds for historical and conceptual analysis, not engineering, safety, construction, or device-operation instructions.
