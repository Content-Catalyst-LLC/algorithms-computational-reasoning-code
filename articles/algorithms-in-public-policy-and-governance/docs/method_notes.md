# Method Notes

This folder treats public algorithms as exercises of administrative power.

The core method is:

1. Define public purpose, legal authority, decision role, and affected people.
2. Map data sources, administrative records, variables, proxies, and vendor systems.
3. Evaluate rights impact, equity, due process, public value, and alternatives.
4. Document decision rules, thresholds, human review, notice, appeal, and remedy.
5. Test performance, subgroup error, data quality, calibration, and failure modes.
6. Publish inventory information and maintain audit trails, logs, and review records.
7. Monitor outcomes, appeals, harms, reliance, drift, and vendor changes.
8. Limit, pause, roll back, or retire systems when safeguards fail.

The examples are dependency-light and educational.
