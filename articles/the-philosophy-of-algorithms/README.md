# The Philosophy of Algorithms

This companion folder supports the Sustainable Catalyst article **The Philosophy of Algorithms**.

It treats algorithms as philosophical objects: procedures, formalizations, abstractions, representations, decision systems, explanations, delegations, limits, institutional artifacts, and governed systems.

## Article sequence

- Previous: `the-history-of-data-structures-and-algorithm-analysis`
- Current: `the-philosophy-of-algorithms`
- Next: `the-future-of-algorithms-computational-reasoning`

## What this folder demonstrates

- how algorithms turn world questions into formal problems, procedures, outputs, and actions;
- why formalization can clarify reality while also omitting context;
- how representation choices shape what an algorithm can see and do;
- why prediction, classification, ranking, recommendation, and optimization require philosophical review;
- how delegation changes agency and responsibility;
- why explanation is audience-specific and cannot substitute for legitimacy;
- how computational limits, uncertainty, Goodhart effects, contestability, and due process matter;
- why AI code generation, agentic workflows, and automated decisions need human judgment, documentation, review, and institutional governance.

All datasets and scores are synthetic and educational. They are not legal advice, ethics certification, model certification, compliance determination, or software safety approval.
