# Rhetorical Algebra Notes

Rhetorical algebra is algebra expressed largely in words rather than modern symbols.

Important interpretive points:

- The absence of modern variables does not mean the absence of abstraction.
- Unknowns can be named in ordinary or technical language.
- Relations can be described verbally.
- Operations can be ordered in prose.
- Worked examples can carry general procedure.
- Geometric demonstration can support correctness.

Rhetorical algebra is not failed symbolism. It is a historical medium of mathematical procedure.
