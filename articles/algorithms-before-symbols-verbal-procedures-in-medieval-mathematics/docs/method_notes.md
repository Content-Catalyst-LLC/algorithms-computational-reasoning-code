# Method Notes

This folder treats verbal procedures as serious mathematical media.

The core method is:

1. Identify the problem type and practical or theoretical context.
2. List named quantities, unknowns, relations, and operations.
3. Extract the ordered verbal instructions.
4. Track intermediate states and transformations.
5. Identify examples, diagrams, or tables that support the rule.
6. Determine how the method is checked or justified.
7. Trace copying, translation, teaching, commentary, or adaptation.
8. Compare with modern symbolic notation only after the historical procedure is understood.

The goal is not to translate everything immediately into modern notation. The goal is to understand the procedure in its historical medium.
