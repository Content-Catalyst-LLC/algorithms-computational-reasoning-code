# Restoration and Balancing Notes

Al-jabr and al-muqabalah are best studied as procedural operations.

## Restoration

Restoration can be interpreted as moving, completing, or transforming a deficient term into a usable form. In modern notation, a rough analogy is adding a quantity to both sides so that a relation becomes easier to solve.

## Balancing

Balancing can be interpreted as comparing, reducing, or canceling comparable terms while preserving the relation. In modern notation, a rough analogy is removing the same quantity from both sides.

These modern formulas are interpretive bridges, not claims that early algebra used modern symbolic notation.
