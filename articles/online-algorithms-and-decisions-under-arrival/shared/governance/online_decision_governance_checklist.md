# Online Algorithm Governance Checklist

- Define what arrives over time and when decisions must be made.
- Record what information was available at decision time.
- State whether the decision is reversible, costly to revise, or final.
- Specify the arrival model: adversarial, random-order, stochastic, nonstationary, strategic, or prediction-augmented.
- Document thresholds, queue rules, cache rules, matching rules, or prediction rules.
- Compare to an offline benchmark, regret baseline, or operational baseline where appropriate.
- Test order sensitivity and fairness under arrival.
- Plan fallback for overload, prediction failure, drift, and adversarial arrivals.
- Preserve logs, timestamps, state, decisions, uncertainty, and appeal/correction records.
- Communicate future-blind limitations plainly.
