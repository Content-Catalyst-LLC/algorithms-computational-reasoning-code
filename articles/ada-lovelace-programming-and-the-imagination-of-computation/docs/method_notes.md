# Method Notes

This folder treats Lovelace as a bridge between mechanical procedure and software imagination.

The core method is:

1. Separate Babbage's machine design from Menabrea's account and Lovelace's notes.
2. Trace Note G as a machine-oriented procedure: target, inputs, operations, intermediate values, and outputs.
3. Distinguish programming concept from modern programming-language syntax.
4. Analyze symbolic generality: number, relation, operation, representation, and domain transfer.
5. Study the music analogy as an argument about pattern and representation.
6. Read machine-limit passages alongside modern AI claims.
7. Map collaboration among Babbage, Menabrea, Lovelace, publishers, historians, and computing communities.
8. Use precise verbs: translated, extended, tabulated, interpreted, imagined, cautioned, and theorized.

The goal is to honor Lovelace without turning her into either a slogan or a dismissal.
