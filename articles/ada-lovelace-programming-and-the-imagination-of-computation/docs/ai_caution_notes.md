# AI Caution Notes

The Lovelace objection is historically rooted in the claim that the Analytical Engine does not originate anything independently; it does what it is ordered to perform.

For modern AI, use this carefully:

- do not claim Lovelace settled all AI debates;
- do ask what systems depend on: architecture, data, objectives, training, prompts, evaluation, and deployment;
- distinguish output from understanding;
- distinguish generation from independent agency;
- keep human responsibility visible.

AI belongs in the toolkit, not in control.
