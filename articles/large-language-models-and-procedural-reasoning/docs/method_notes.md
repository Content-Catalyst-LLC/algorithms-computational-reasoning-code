# Method Notes

This companion workflow frames LLM outputs as reviewable procedural artifacts. The central question is not whether an output sounds fluent. The central question is whether the workflow has enough structure, evidence, verification, and governance for its intended use.

## Review questions

1. What task did the model appear to perform?
2. What are the stakes of the output?
3. Does the response include enough procedural structure for review?
4. Which factual claims require citations or retrieved evidence?
5. Does the output use risky certainty language?
6. Were any tools, files, databases, or external systems involved?
7. Who verifies the output before consequential use?
8. What use boundaries and escalation rules apply?

## Recommended artifacts

- prompt record;
- source-grounding table;
- tool-permission matrix;
- factuality and citation audit;
- risk-language review;
- human-review assignment;
- escalation and incident log;
- use-boundary statement.
