# Method Notes

This folder treats health algorithms as care-system and public-health interventions.

The core method is:

1. Define health purpose, decision role, patient or population impact, setting, and workflow.
2. Map data sources, labels, missingness, access barriers, privacy constraints, and proxy variables.
3. Validate model performance, calibration, subgroup error, clinical usefulness, and actionability.
4. Assess patient safety, public-health impact, workflow burden, alert fatigue, and failure modes.
5. Define human review, override authority, escalation, explanation, correction, and remedy.
6. Implement privacy, security, access control, purpose limitation, and data-governance safeguards.
7. Monitor outcomes, drift, errors, overrides, inequity, burden, and unintended consequences.
8. Pause, limit, roll back, or retire systems when unsafe, inequitable, or clinically unhelpful.

The examples are dependency-light and educational.
