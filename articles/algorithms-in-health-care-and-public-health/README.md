# Algorithms in Health Care and Public Health

This companion folder supports the Sustainable Catalyst article **Algorithms in Health Care and Public Health**.

It provides reproducible, teaching-oriented workflows for clinical decision support, diagnostic models, triage, risk stratification, electronic health records, public-health surveillance, outbreak detection, resource allocation, population health, claims review, health-equity auditing, privacy safeguards, clinical validation, monitoring, audit trails, and responsible health algorithm governance.

## Article sequence

- Previous: `algorithms-in-finance-markets-and-risk`
- Current: `algorithms-in-health-care-and-public-health`
- Next: `algorithms-in-climate-energy-and-infrastructure`

## What this folder demonstrates

- how health algorithm systems can be reviewed for patient impact, population impact, clinical validation, equity readiness, privacy readiness, human review, workflow integration, monitoring, and governance readiness;
- how diagnostic performance, risk thresholds, public-health capacity, health algorithm risk, and health governance readiness can be calculated in small examples;
- how high-impact clinical systems can require clinical safety review;
- how public-health systems can require public-health governance review;
- how health algorithm governance requires inventories, validation reports, equity review, privacy review, human review protocols, monitoring, audit trails, and stop rules;
- how calculators, tests, SQL queries, Canvas cards, and multilingual examples can support responsible health algorithm governance.

## Repository structure

- `python/` — reference health algorithm safety audit workflow and tests
- `r/` — diagnostic summaries and figures
- `sql/` — schema and queries for health systems, safety scores, and governance controls
- `calculators/` — self-contained sensitivity, specificity, risk-threshold, governance-readiness, health-risk, and SIR-style calculators
- `canvas/` — Canvas-ready cards, schemas, and exports
- `docs/` — method notes, data dictionary, equity review notes, privacy notes, validation notes, governance review, and use boundaries
- language folders — compact examples across Python, R, Julia, SQL, Haskell, Rust, Go, C, C++, Fortran, Java, TypeScript, Prolog, and Racket

All datasets and examples are synthetic and educational. They are not medical advice, clinical advice, public-health advice, legal advice, regulatory compliance advice, privacy advice, safety certification, or operational guidance.
