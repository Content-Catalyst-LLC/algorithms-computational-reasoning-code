# Method Notes

This folder treats Wiener as a theorist of feedback, control, communication, prediction, automation ethics, and governance.

The core method is:

1. Define cybernetics as control and communication, not merely "cyber."
2. Identify the feedback loop: observe, compare, decide, act, return.
3. Distinguish negative feedback from positive feedback.
4. Trace error signals, delay, noise, and correction.
5. Study prediction and wartime control as historical context.
6. Compare animals, machines, software, platforms, and institutions carefully.
7. Analyze automation ethics through human agency and responsibility.
8. Apply feedback-loop analysis to AI systems, platforms, and institutional decisions.

The goal is to govern the loop, not only the model.
