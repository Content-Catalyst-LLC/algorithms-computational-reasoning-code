# Algorithmic Trust Governance Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing a computational system whose outputs, artifacts, controls, or decisions require justified confidence.

1. Define the bounded trust claim: trusted for what purpose, in what context, and under what assumptions.
2. Specify expected behavior, limits, prohibited uses, decision authority, and stop conditions.
3. Gather verification evidence showing the system satisfies its specification.
4. Gather validation evidence showing the system fits the real-world purpose and population.
5. Review security controls across confidentiality, integrity, availability, authentication, authorization, and accountability.
6. Preserve provenance for data, code, model artifacts, parameters, configurations, reviews, and releases.
7. Verify artifacts through hashes, signatures, manifests, controlled registries, and reproducible workflows where appropriate.
8. Monitor drift, errors, incidents, false positives, misuse, and changes after deployment.
9. Maintain residual-risk registers, escalation paths, incident-response plans, and remediation records.
10. Calibrate human reliance with uncertainty, limits, guidance, and appeal or review pathways.
11. Document governance owners, evidence requirements, approval criteria, change-control rules, and retirement criteria.
12. Communicate trust claims precisely without treating tests, audits, encryption, or automation as complete proof of trustworthiness.
