# Method Notes

This folder treats geography, coordinates, and mapping as spatial algorithmic reasoning.

The core method is:

1. Identify the map, coordinate list, route text, gazetteer, or geographical description.
2. Extract place names, feature types, coordinates, routes, regions, or descriptions.
3. Identify the reference frame: latitude, longitude, climate zone, orientation, or route order.
4. Determine the procedure: lookup, plotting, correction, route sequencing, projection, or classification.
5. Track corrections, inconsistencies, omissions, and possible source conflicts.
6. Ask who uses the representation: scholar, traveler, administrator, merchant, jurist, teacher, or ruler.
7. Study copying, translation, redrawing, commentary, and localization.
8. Compare with modern geospatial computation only after understanding the historical representation.

The goal is to treat maps and coordinate lists as structured artifacts rather than neutral pictures.
