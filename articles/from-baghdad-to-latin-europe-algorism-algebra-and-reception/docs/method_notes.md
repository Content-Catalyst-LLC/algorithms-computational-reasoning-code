# Method Notes

This folder treats mathematical reception as active computational knowledge transfer.

The core method is:

1. Identify the method: algorism, algebra, table use, commercial arithmetic, or notation system.
2. Map the transfer pathway: Arabic, Latin, Hebrew, vernacular, school, merchant, or university.
3. Identify representation change: numerals, notation, terminology, diagrams, or examples.
4. Study teaching format: rule, worked example, problem book, commentary, or practical manual.
5. Ask where the method is used: commerce, astronomy, inheritance, surveying, schooling, or administration.
6. Identify trust mechanisms: verification, checking, record conventions, institutional approval, or repetition.
7. Trace resistance and mixed practice rather than assuming immediate replacement.
8. Avoid single-hero or single-origin stories by mapping networks.

The goal is to treat reception as active transformation, not passive borrowing.
