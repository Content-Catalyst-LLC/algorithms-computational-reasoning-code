# Method Notes

This folder treats frequency analysis as early algorithmic inference over language.

The core method is:

1. Identify the cipher type and whether a simple substitution assumption is plausible.
2. Identify the likely language, genre, and writing conventions.
3. Count symbols in the ciphertext.
4. Rank symbols and compare them with expected plaintext frequencies.
5. Test substitutions by producing partial plaintext.
6. Use repeated patterns, word lengths, and linguistic constraints to refine guesses.
7. Revise when evidence conflicts.
8. Ask how the method is used, taught, limited, and ethically bounded.

The goal is to keep al-Kindī’s work visible as disciplined historical inference: count, compare, hypothesize, test, revise, and interpret.
