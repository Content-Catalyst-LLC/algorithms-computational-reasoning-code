# Classification Governance Checklist

- Is the decision purpose explicit?
- Are categories and labels defined?
- Are features documented and justified?
- Is the threshold rationale recorded?
- Are scores calibrated or clearly described as non-probabilistic?
- Are false positives and false negatives defined in domain terms?
- Are error costs reviewed?
- Are group-level and contextual distributional effects audited?
- Are borderline cases routed to review when appropriate?
- Is there an exception and appeal process?
- Are rule and model versions preserved?
- Can the decision be reconstructed from evidence to label to consequence?
