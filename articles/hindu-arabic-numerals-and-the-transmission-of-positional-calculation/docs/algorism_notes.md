# Algorism Notes

Algorism refers to medieval traditions of written calculation using Hindu-Arabic numerals. The term is historically connected to Latin reception of al-Khwārizmī's name.

Important distinctions:

- Algorism is historically narrower than modern algorithm.
- Algorism centers on written arithmetic with positional numerals.
- Algorithm later generalizes toward finite rule-governed procedure.
- Written reckoning made arithmetic visible, teachable, copyable, and checkable.

Algorism belongs in computational history because it shows arithmetic becoming a documented procedure.
