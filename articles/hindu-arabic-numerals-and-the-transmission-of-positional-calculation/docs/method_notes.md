# Method Notes

This folder treats Hindu-Arabic numerals as representational, procedural, pedagogical, practical, and translational infrastructure for algorithmic reasoning.

The core method is:

1. Distinguish digit shapes, place-value structure, zero, and arithmetic procedures.
2. Identify Indian mathematical origins and Arabic/Islamic-world transmission.
3. Study how procedures were taught in arithmetic and algorism texts.
4. Examine practical domains: commerce, accounting, astronomy, administration, and education.
5. Compare written calculation with abacus, counting board, mental arithmetic, and tabular methods.
6. Track Latin reception and later standardization.
7. Explain modern computational relevance without collapsing history into modern computer science.
8. Avoid erasing either Indian origins or Arabic/Islamic-world adaptation and transmission.

The examples are dependency-light and educational.
