# Oversight Notes

Human-in-the-loop is not automatically meaningful oversight.

A reviewer needs context, time, authority, training, uncertainty information, and practical ability to override.
Acceptance rates should be compared with context-specific model quality.
High acceptance can indicate appropriate trust, but it can also indicate automation bias or workflow pressure.
Low override rates can indicate strong model performance, but they can also indicate friction, fear, or rubber-stamping.
Appeals and error reports are evidence about whether contestability works in practice.
