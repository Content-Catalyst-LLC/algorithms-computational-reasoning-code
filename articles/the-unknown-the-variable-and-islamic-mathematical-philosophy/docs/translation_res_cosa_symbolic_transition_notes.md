# Translation: Res, Cosa, and Symbolic Transition Notes

The unknown moved across languages.

Important stages:

- Arabic verbal algebra names the unknown as a thing/root;
- Latin reception uses terms such as res;
- vernacular traditions use terms such as cosa;
- cossic algebra preserves the unknown as a named object;
- symbolic algebra later uses compact letter notation;
- modern mathematics distinguishes unknowns, variables, parameters, and indeterminates.

The history of the variable is also a history of translation.
