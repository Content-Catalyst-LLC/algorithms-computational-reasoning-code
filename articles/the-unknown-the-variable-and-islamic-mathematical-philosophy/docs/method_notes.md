# Method Notes

This folder treats the unknown as a disciplined mathematical object.

The core method is:

1. Identify the sought quantity in the problem.
2. Record how the unknown is named: thing, root, share, side, amount, or another term.
3. Classify the problem case: square, root, number, or combined relation.
4. Trace transformations: restoration, balancing, reduction, completion, extraction.
5. Identify the proof form: example, geometric construction, arithmetic check, or commentary.
6. Ask what kind of object the unknown is: number, magnitude, share, debt, or formal placeholder.
7. Trace translation: Arabic thing, Latin res, vernacular cosa, symbolic x, or later variable.
8. Avoid projecting modern variable notation backward.

The goal is to study the unknown as a bridge between absence, representation, proof, and procedure.
