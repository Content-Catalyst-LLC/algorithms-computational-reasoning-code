# Data Dictionary

## unknown_variable_themes.csv

- `theme_id`: interpretive theme in the unknown-variable-philosophy map.
- `unknown_representation`: importance of naming, representing, and stabilizing the unknown.
- `procedural_transformation`: importance of restoration, balancing, completion, reduction, extraction, and verification.
- `abstraction`: importance of treating quantities beyond a single numerical instance.
- `proof_relation`: importance of demonstration, correctness, and justification.
- `translation_continuity`: importance of Arabic, Latin, vernacular, and symbolic transmission.
- `practical_grounding`: importance of inheritance, trade, surveying, debt, geometry, astronomy, and other applied settings.
- `philosophical_depth`: importance for questions about number, magnitude, abstraction, method, and certainty.
- `historical_significance`: importance for the history of algebra and algorithmic reasoning.
- `ethical_caution`: importance of avoiding anachronism, single-origin stories, and notation-based prejudice.
- `modern_resonance`: degree to which the theme clarifies variables, parameters, missing data, latent states, and model targets today.

## unknown_variable_map.csv

- `unknown_variable_score`: average of the theme dimensions.
- `interpretive_status`: core, major, or supporting unknown-variable-philosophy thread.

## interpretation_cautions.csv

- `caution`: interpretive risk to avoid.
- `meaning`: explanation of the caution.
