# Method Notes

This folder treats human judgment as a designed institutional capacity.

The core method is:

1. Classify decision stakes, uncertainty, reversibility, and affected-person consequences.
2. Define the human role: reviewer, approver, investigator, corrector, escalator, or decision authority.
3. Evaluate review capacity: time, information, authority, training, and protection.
4. Measure reliance, override rate, escalation capacity, contestability, and governance readiness.
5. Estimate review risk when stakes are high and judgment capacity is weak.
6. Identify contexts where human review is meaningful, symbolic, or unsafe.
7. Connect human review records to appeals, correction, remediation, and system learning.

The examples are dependency-light and educational.
