# Counterfactual Review Checklist

Use this checklist before presenting a counterfactual explanation as meaningful or actionable.

## Counterfactual definition

- What decision, score, threshold, rule, or institutional outcome is being explained?
- What input, rule, threshold, or condition is changed in the counterfactual?
- Is the counterfactual local to one case or general to a group?
- Is the comparison condition explicit?

## Causal and temporal validity

- Could the changed feature plausibly occur before the decision?
- Is the changed feature causally upstream of the outcome?
- Are post-decision or post-treatment variables being changed incorrectly?
- Does the counterfactual violate known constraints, timing, or policy rules?

## Feasibility and recourse

- Is the proposed change within the person or institution's control?
- Is the change affordable, safe, legal, and realistic?
- Does the explanation ask someone to change protected, historical, immutable, or structurally constrained attributes?
- Is there more than one path to a better outcome?

## Governance

- Is the original decision rule documented?
- Can affected people contest the explanation?
- Are structural constraints distinguished from individual responsibility?
- Is the explanation understandable to the audience that needs it?
- Is counterfactual reasoning being used to clarify accountability rather than shift blame?
