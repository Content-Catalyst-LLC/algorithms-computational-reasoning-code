# Governance Notes

Counterfactual explanations are often attractive because they sound practical: "If X had been different, the decision would have changed." But the governance burden is higher than that simple sentence suggests.

## Risks

1. **Unrealistic recourse** — A system may propose changes that cannot be achieved.
2. **Blame shifting** — Structural or institutional constraints may be reframed as individual failure.
3. **Proxy manipulation** — A counterfactual may advise changing a proxy rather than the real underlying condition.
4. **Causal confusion** — Changing a feature in a model does not prove that changing it in the world will change the outcome.
5. **Contestability gap** — Affected people may receive explanations without a meaningful appeal path.

## Minimum documentation

- Decision rule or model summary.
- Threshold or action boundary.
- Features permitted for counterfactual change.
- Features excluded from recourse.
- Feasibility and actionability review.
- Known limits, uncertainty, and appeal process.
