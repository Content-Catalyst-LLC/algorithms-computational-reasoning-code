# Optimization, Objectives, and Constraints Governance Checklist

- Define the decision being supported and why optimization is appropriate.
- Specify decision variables and distinguish them from parameters and observed data.
- Document the objective function and explain its relationship to the real-world purpose.
- Inventory hard constraints, soft constraints, penalties, and regularization terms.
- Define the feasible set and test whether it is empty, overbroad, or overnarrow.
- Review data quality, parameter uncertainty, and assumptions.
- Analyze trade-offs among cost, quality, risk, fairness, robustness, and public value.
- Test sensitivity to objective weights, constraints, costs, demand estimates, thresholds, and uncertainty ranges.
- Evaluate distributional effects across affected groups or regions.
- Preserve optimization traces, solver settings, candidate comparisons, and audit evidence.
- Define appeal, review, and override mechanisms where decisions affect people or institutions.
