# Method Notes

This folder treats financial algorithms as governed risk systems.

The core method is:

1. Define financial purpose, decision role, affected parties, instruments, and market context.
2. Map data sources, variables, assumptions, thresholds, model scope, and permitted use.
3. Evaluate consumer impact, market impact, liquidity risk, model risk, and operational risk.
4. Validate conceptual soundness, data quality, implementation, performance, and sensitivity.
5. Stress test adverse scenarios, tail losses, liquidity shocks, feedback loops, and operational failures.
6. Define human review, overrides, escalation, adverse-action explanation, appeal, and remediation.
7. Monitor drift, errors, losses, customer harm, market impact, and control failures.
8. Pause, limit, roll back, or retire systems when controls fail or conditions change.

The examples are dependency-light and educational.
