# Algorithms in Finance, Markets, and Risk

This companion folder supports the Sustainable Catalyst article **Algorithms in Finance, Markets, and Risk**.

It provides reproducible, teaching-oriented workflows for credit scoring, fraud detection, algorithmic trading, order routing, market microstructure, liquidity, portfolio optimization, risk modeling, value at risk, stress testing, systemic risk, consumer finance, model validation, audit trails, and responsible financial algorithm governance.

## Article sequence

- Previous: `algorithms-in-media-platforms-and-attention-systems`
- Current: `algorithms-in-finance-markets-and-risk`
- Next: `algorithms-in-health-care-and-public-health`

## What this folder demonstrates

- how financial algorithm systems can be reviewed for market impact, consumer impact, model risk, transparency, human review, validation, monitoring, governance readiness, and liquidity risk;
- how expected loss, portfolio risk, value at risk, stress loss, governance readiness, and financial algorithm risk can be calculated in small examples;
- how high-impact consumer-finance systems can require consumer-protection review;
- how high-impact market systems can require market-stability review;
- how financial algorithm governance requires model inventories, validation reports, stress tests, audit trails, consumer review, market controls, monitoring, and stop rules;
- how calculators, tests, SQL queries, Canvas cards, and multilingual examples can support responsible financial risk governance.

## Repository structure

- `python/` — reference financial algorithm risk audit workflow and tests
- `r/` — diagnostic summaries and figures
- `sql/` — schema and queries for financial systems, risk scores, and governance controls
- `calculators/` — self-contained expected-loss, portfolio-return, portfolio-risk, VaR, stress-loss, governance-readiness, and financial-risk calculators
- `canvas/` — Canvas-ready cards, schemas, and exports
- `docs/` — method notes, data dictionary, model-risk notes, stress-testing notes, governance review, and use boundaries
- language folders — compact examples across Python, R, Julia, SQL, Haskell, Rust, Go, C, C++, Fortran, Java, TypeScript, Prolog, and Racket

All datasets and examples are synthetic and educational. They are not investment advice, trading advice, credit advice, legal advice, regulatory compliance advice, consumer finance advice, accounting advice, or operational risk certification.
