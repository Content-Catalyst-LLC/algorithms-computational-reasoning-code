# Labor Impact Assessment

Labor-impact assessment should review:

- hiring access;
- screening criteria;
- pay and incentive effects;
- scheduling stability;
- workload distribution;
- task pace;
- safety and fatigue;
- surveillance burden;
- discipline and deactivation;
- promotion and development;
- worker autonomy;
- worker voice and contestability.

A workplace algorithm should not improve efficiency by hiding human strain or shifting unmanaged risk onto workers.
