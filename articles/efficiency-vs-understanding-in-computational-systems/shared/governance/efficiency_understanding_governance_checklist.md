# Efficiency and Understanding Governance Checklist

- Define the efficiency claim: faster, smaller, cheaper, lower energy, more scalable, or more automated.
- Specify the baseline workload, measurement method, and resource metric.
- Document what changed in the algorithm, model, data, infrastructure, or workflow.
- Identify what became harder to read, debug, explain, observe, audit, reproduce, or maintain.
- Test edge cases, failure modes, subgroup effects, stale data, and degraded-quality modes.
- Preserve provenance for data, code, models, parameters, deployments, and outputs.
- Pair performance metrics with interpretability and governance metrics.
- Define minimum understanding requirements for consequential use.
- Provide escalation, override, appeal, correction, and stop mechanisms.
- Communicate trade-offs plainly rather than presenting efficiency as neutral improvement.
