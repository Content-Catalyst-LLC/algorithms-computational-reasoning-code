# Data Quality, Missingness, and Computational Judgment Governance Checklist

- Define intended computational use before judging data quality.
- Inventory sources, collection limits, access limits, and update timing.
- Profile missingness by field, source, group, geography, category, and time period.
- Classify missingness reasons: unknown, not applicable, not collected, withheld, pending, invalid, redacted, or structural zero.
- Preserve missingness indicators and imputation flags.
- Validate completeness, validity, uniqueness, consistency, freshness, integrity, provenance, and schema stability.
- Review representativeness and coverage gaps before generalizing.
- Separate observed values from inferred or imputed values.
- Communicate uncertainty, exclusions, stale data, and coverage limits.
- Add governance gates when validation, provenance, representativeness, or missingness thresholds fail.
