# Word, Concept, and Practice Layers

Do not collapse these layers:

- Word history: the etymology of algorithm, algorism, algebra, and related terms.
- Concept history: the emergence of generalized ideas of procedure.
- Practice history: older traditions of stepwise calculation, proof, tables, instruments, and administration.
- Transmission history: translation, copying, commentary, teaching, reception, and institutional memory.
- Modern formalization: logic, computability, programming, machine execution, and computer science.

The layers overlap but do not begin at the same point.
