# Method Notes

This folder treats Islamic-world roots of algorithmic reasoning as procedural, translational, practical, representational, and institutional history.

The core method is:

1. Define the type of algorithmic reasoning being studied: arithmetic, algebra, table, instrument, cryptanalysis, mechanical sequence, or practical calculation.
2. Separate etymology from broader intellectual history.
3. Identify earlier sources, translations, adaptations, commentaries, and later transmissions.
4. Examine procedure, not only result.
5. Study representations: numerals, prose rules, diagrams, tables, instruments, and devices.
6. Connect practical domains to scholarly method.
7. Avoid modern projection while explaining modern relevance.
8. Place Islamic-world contributions within multi-civilizational history without minimizing originality.

The examples are dependency-light and educational.
