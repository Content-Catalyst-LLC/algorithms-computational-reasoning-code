# Transmission Notes

Transmission is a form of intellectual work.

Translation, commentary, adaptation, correction, terminology creation, table compilation, example selection, pedagogical reorganization, and institutional teaching all shape how procedures survive and move across regions.

For Islamic-world algorithmic reasoning, transmission includes:

- Greek, Syriac, Persian, Sanskrit, and Arabic scholarly exchange;
- Arabic mathematical and astronomical cultures;
- practical calculation traditions;
- Latin translations and adaptations;
- algorism texts;
- abacus schools and commercial arithmetic;
- later algebraic and computational traditions.

Algorithmic history is a history of movement, not only invention.
