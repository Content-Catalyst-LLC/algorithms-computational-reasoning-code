# Origin Story Cautions

Careful historical interpretation should avoid:

- saying algorithms began with one person;
- confusing the etymology of algorithm with the total origin of procedural reasoning;
- describing medieval procedures as if they were modern computer programs;
- treating Islamic-world scholarship as merely preservation;
- treating translation as passive copying;
- turning multi-civilizational exchange into a ranking exercise;
- ignoring practical domains such as commerce, inheritance, surveying, astronomy, geography, and cryptanalysis;
- ignoring scribes, translators, teachers, practitioners, instruments, tables, and institutions.

The strongest account is appreciative, precise, and historically cautious.
