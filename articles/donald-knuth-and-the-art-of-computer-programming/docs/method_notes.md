# Method Notes

This folder treats Knuth as a theorist of algorithmic analysis, rigorous exposition, literate programming, typography, and computational craft.

The core method is:

1. Begin with TAOCP as a model of algorithmic exposition.
2. Identify the problem, procedure, data representation, and assumptions.
3. Analyze time, space, average case, worst case, and growth.
4. Study sorting, searching, random numbers, and data structures as foundational cases.
5. Use MIX/MMIX to understand machine-model assumptions.
6. Study TeX and METAFONT as computational typography.
7. Study literate programming as executable explanation.
8. Apply Knuthian review to AI-generated code.

The goal is to treat algorithms as artifacts of analysis, style, proof, and explanation.
