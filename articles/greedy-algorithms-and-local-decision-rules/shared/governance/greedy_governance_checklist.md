# Greedy Algorithm Governance Checklist

- Define the global objective and local decision rule.
- Label proof status: exact, bounded approximation, heuristic, or policy rule.
- Document greedy-choice evidence, exchange argument, approximation bound, or empirical validation.
- Define feasibility checks, tie-breaking rules, and stopping conditions.
- Test edge cases, counterexamples, missing values, ties, negative weights, and adversarial inputs.
- Preserve local-decision traces: candidate set, scores, selected item, timestamp, and rejection reasons.
- Monitor short-term bias, proxy optimization, exposure effects, deferred cases, and feedback loops.
- Define human review, override, rollback, and change-control procedures.
