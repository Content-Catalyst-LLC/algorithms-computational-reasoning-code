# Big-O Claim Governance Checklist

- Define what input size means.
- State the resource being analyzed: time, memory, I/O, communication, cost, or human review.
- State whether the claim is best, average, worst, amortized, or empirical.
- Explain the dominant term and omitted lower-order terms.
- Distinguish Big-O, Big-Theta, and Big-Omega.
- Provide benchmark evidence where practical.
- Estimate scalability thresholds under relevant budgets.
- Review hidden costs: memory, preprocessing, I/O, networking, constants, and governance workload.
- Communicate claims in plain language.
- Avoid vague terms like "fast" or "efficient" without evidence.
