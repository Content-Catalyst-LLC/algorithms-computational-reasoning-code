# Method Notes

This folder treats automation as delegated authority.

The core method is:

1. Define the task, affected people, decision stakes, and institutional purpose.
2. Identify what authority is being delegated: sorting, prediction, ranking, recommendation, triage, final action, or monitoring.
3. Assess evidence quality, validation, reversibility, contestability, governance, and human review.
4. Measure automation reliance to detect when support becomes de facto decision-making.
5. Estimate delegation risk when stakes are high and readiness is weak.
6. Recommend delegation mode: no automation, information support, decision support, constrained automation, conditional automation, or full automation.
7. Define escalation, rollback, appeal, remediation, and retirement controls.

The examples are dependency-light and educational.
