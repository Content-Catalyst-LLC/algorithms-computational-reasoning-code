# Method Notes

This folder treats al-Khwārizmī as a decisive historical bridge in algorithmic method.

The core method is:

1. Separate word history from method history.
2. Identify which work is being discussed: arithmetic, algebra, astronomy, or geography.
3. Analyze representation: numerals, verbal rules, cases, diagrams, or tables.
4. Trace procedural structure: input, case, rule, transformation, result, verification.
5. Ask what earlier traditions are involved: Indian numeration, Greek geometry, Arabic scholarship, Persian context, and others.
6. Trace reception: Arabic manuscript culture, Latin translation, algorism, schools, and public memory.
7. Mark modern analogies carefully.
8. Use precise verbs: systematized, explained, transmitted, adapted, organized, formalized.

The goal is to honor al-Khwārizmī precisely without turning him into a slogan.
