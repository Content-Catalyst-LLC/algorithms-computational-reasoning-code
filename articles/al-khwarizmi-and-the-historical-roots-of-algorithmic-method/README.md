# Al-Khwārizmī and the Historical Roots of Algorithmic Method

This companion folder supports the Sustainable Catalyst article **Al-Khwārizmī and the Historical Roots of Algorithmic Method**.

It provides reproducible, teaching-oriented workflows for studying al-Khwārizmī as a decisive bridge in algorithmic method: algorism, written calculation, Hindu-Arabic numerals, place value, algebraic case classification, al-jabr, al-muqābalah, unknowns, roots, squares, numbers, geometric demonstration, astronomical and geographical tables, Arabic scientific culture, Latin reception, etymology, and careful historiography.

## What this folder demonstrates

- how al-Khwārizmī belongs at the center of algorithm word history without being reduced to a mythic inventor of every algorithm;
- how algorism made written calculation with Hindu-Arabic numerals portable and teachable;
- how place value, zero, carrying, borrowing, and checking function as algorithmic arithmetic;
- how algebraic problem solving organizes unknowns, roots, squares, numbers, cases, rules, transformations, and verification;
- how al-jabr and al-muqābalah function as historical names for procedural transformation;
- how geometric demonstration relates procedure to correctness;
- how astronomical and geographical tables preserve structured computational method;
- how Latin reception and translation shaped the movement from algorism to algorithm;
- how to distinguish precise historical legacy from slogan, anachronism, and etymology-only explanation.

## Repository structure

- `python/` — reference al-Khwārizmī algorithmic-method map workflow and tests
- `r/` — historical roots diagnostics and figures
- `sql/` — schema and queries for themes, method scores, and cautions
- `calculators/` — self-contained algorism, place-value, equation-case, transformation, verification, etymology, and myth-caution calculators
- `canvas/` — Canvas-ready cards, schemas, and exports
- `docs/` — method notes, data dictionary, al-Khwārizmī notes, algorism notes, algebra notes, reception notes, and use boundaries
- language folders — compact examples across Python, R, Julia, SQL, Haskell, Rust, Go, C, C++, Fortran, Java, TypeScript, Prolog, and Racket

All datasets and examples are synthetic and interpretive. They are educational scaffolds for historical and conceptual analysis, not claims about a single manuscript lineage or authoritative chronology.
