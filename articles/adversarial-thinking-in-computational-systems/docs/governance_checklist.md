# Adversarial Thinking Governance Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing a computational system that may face strategic behavior, abuse, manipulation, evasion, overload, or misuse.

1. Define the assets, functions, people, records, models, services, and institutional purposes that need protection.
2. Identify likely adversaries, including outsiders, insiders, bots, strategic users, competitors, abusive users, and accidental misuse patterns.
3. Map attack surfaces across interfaces, APIs, prompts, data pipelines, models, dependencies, human workflows, and governance processes.
4. Mark trust boundaries where data, authority, identity, instructions, or control crosses contexts.
5. Write abuse cases and misuse paths for ordinary features.
6. Test evasion, gaming, adversarial examples, prompt injection, data poisoning, and overload scenarios where relevant.
7. Inventory preventive, detective, containment, recovery, and accountability controls.
8. Review false positives, legitimate-user burden, and disparate impact from defensive controls.
9. Define monitoring, alerting, escalation, incident response, rollback, and recovery responsibilities.
10. Maintain red-team findings, remediation records, retesting results, and threat-model updates.
11. Communicate residual risk without exposing sensitive defensive details.
12. Assign ownership for adversarial review across the system lifecycle.
