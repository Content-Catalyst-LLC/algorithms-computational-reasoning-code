# Intervention Modeling Method Notes

Causal algorithms and intervention modeling connect formal causal reasoning to operational decision support. The basic workflow is:

1. Define the intervention.
2. Define the comparison condition.
3. Identify the outcome.
4. Specify causal assumptions.
5. Review confounding and adjustment.
6. Estimate effects under clear scenarios.
7. Test sensitivity to model choices.
8. Evaluate feasibility, cost, harm, distribution, and governance.
9. State the boundary of use.

## Separation of tasks

- **Causal identification** asks whether the effect can be learned under assumptions.
- **Estimation** computes a numerical effect.
- **Optimization** compares intervention choices.
- **Governance** decides whether the modeled action should be used.

These are related but not interchangeable.
