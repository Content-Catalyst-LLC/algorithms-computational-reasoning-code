# Privacy-Preserving Computation Governance Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing a privacy-preserving computation workflow.

1. Define the purpose of the computation.
2. Inventory sensitive data, identifiers, quasi-identifiers, local records, outputs, models, logs, and intermediate artifacts.
3. Document the threat model.
4. Choose a method that fits the privacy goal.
5. Document parameters such as epsilon, delta, aggregation thresholds, protocol assumptions, keys, and participant sets.
6. Track privacy-budget composition where relevant.
7. Review output leakage and small-cell disclosure risk.
8. Validate statistical utility and downstream decision fitness.
9. Review fairness and group-level distortion.
10. Preserve privacy-aware audit records.
11. Plan incident response and remediation.
12. Communicate what is protected, what remains exposed, and under which assumptions.
