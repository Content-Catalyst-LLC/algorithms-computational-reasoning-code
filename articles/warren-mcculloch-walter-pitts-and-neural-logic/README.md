# Warren McCulloch, Walter Pitts, and Neural Logic

This companion folder supports the Sustainable Catalyst article **Warren McCulloch, Walter Pitts, and Neural Logic**.

It provides reproducible, teaching-oriented workflows for studying Warren McCulloch, Walter Pitts, neural logic, the McCulloch-Pitts neuron, threshold logic, Boolean networks, cybernetics, automata theory, artificial intelligence history, biological abstraction, and responsible model interpretation.

## What this folder demonstrates

- how simplified threshold units can represent logical operations;
- why the McCulloch-Pitts neuron is a formal abstraction rather than a full biological neuron;
- how neural logic bridges symbolic logic, nervous activity, automata, cybernetics, and AI;
- why neural and symbolic traditions were historically intertwined;
- how the lineage connects to perceptrons, connectionism, neural networks, hybrid AI, and representation learning;
- why responsible AI requires careful abstraction boundaries and resistance to overclaiming.

All datasets and examples are synthetic and educational. They are not neuroscience validation, model certification, production AI guidance, medical advice, or safety approval.
