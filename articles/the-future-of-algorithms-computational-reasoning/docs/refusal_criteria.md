# Refusal Criteria

Responsible algorithmic futures include principled refusal.

Delay, redesign, limit, or refuse deployment when:

- the problem is poorly defined;
- data are invalid, unjust, or untraceable;
- uncertainty is high and unstated;
- the decision is high-stakes and opaque;
- affected people have no appeal;
- humans cannot meaningfully review;
- the metric is easy to game;
- the institution cannot monitor, audit, or repair harm.

A no-go decision is a valid result of computational reasoning.
