# The Future of Algorithms & Computational Reasoning

This companion folder supports the Sustainable Catalyst capstone article **The Future of Algorithms & Computational Reasoning**.

It provides reproducible, teaching-oriented workflows for studying future algorithmic systems as technical, institutional, and human-governed systems. The emphasis is not on prediction hype, but on responsible procedural power: AI systems, agents, scientific computing, simulation, optimization, uncertainty, software infrastructure, public institutions, platforms, applied domains, generated code, computational literacy, and principled refusal.

## What this folder demonstrates

- how future algorithmic systems combine models, data, objectives, infrastructure, and governance;
- why technical capability is not the same as deployment readiness;
- how AI agents and tool-using systems require permission boundaries, audit trails, and rollback;
- why scientific algorithms need uncertainty, sensitivity analysis, reproducibility, and validation;
- how public institutions, platforms, health, finance, climate, education, and labor require domain-specific governance;
- why generated code still requires review, testing, documentation, and ownership;
- how computational literacy must include data, algorithms, statistics, systems, AI limits, and governance;
- why some systems should be delayed, redesigned, or refused.

All datasets and examples are synthetic and educational. They are not legal advice, model certification, compliance determination, risk approval, production benchmarks, or safety assurance.
