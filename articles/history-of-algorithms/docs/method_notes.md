# Method Notes

This folder treats algorithm history as a layered movement from procedure to computation.

The core method is:

1. Identify the procedure and its problem context.
2. Determine its representation: prose, example, diagram, table, notation, machine, or program.
3. Ask what counts as correctness: proof, check, example, test, or institutional trust.
4. Trace transmission: teaching, copying, translation, commentary, printing, programming, or deployment.
5. Identify execution environment: human, instrument, machine, software, platform, or institution.
6. Analyze constraints: time, memory, materials, notation, data, governance, or cost.
7. Mark analogies carefully when using modern terms for older procedures.
8. Ask who benefits, who is affected, and who can contest the procedure.

The goal is to include both ancient procedure and modern computation without collapsing one into the other.
