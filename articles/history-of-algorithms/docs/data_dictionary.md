# Data Dictionary

## algorithm_history_layers.csv

- `layer_id`: interpretive historical layer in the algorithm history map.
- `procedural_explicitness`: degree to which steps, rules, cases, or operations are explicit.
- `representation`: importance of notation, diagrams, tables, symbols, machines, or code.
- `proof_correctness`: degree to which correctness, demonstration, verification, or testing is central.
- `portability`: degree to which the method travels across contexts, media, languages, or institutions.
- `mechanization`: degree to which procedure is embodied in instruments, devices, machines, or automated systems.
- `formalization`: degree to which the procedure is generalized, symbolized, or mathematically formalized.
- `programmability`: degree to which procedure can be expressed for machine execution.
- `institutional_adoption`: degree to which schools, commerce, science, administration, software, or platforms adopt the method.
- `governance_relevance`: importance for accountability, audit, contestability, public trust, and responsibility.
- `modern_resonance`: degree to which the layer clarifies current algorithmic systems.

## algorithm_history_map.csv

- `history_score`: average of the layer dimensions.
- `interpretive_status`: core, major, or supporting algorithm-history layer.

## interpretation_cautions.csv

- `caution`: interpretive risk to avoid.
- `meaning`: explanation of the caution.
