# Graph Search, Pathfinding, and Routing Governance Checklist

- Define nodes, edges, direction, labels, weights, metadata, and update cadence.
- Document what edge weights mean: distance, time, latency, risk, price, energy, or composite score.
- Identify blocked, restricted, risky, stale, or uncertain nodes and edges.
- Preserve path traces, parent pointers, cost records, and explored-set summaries.
- Compare selected routes with plausible alternatives.
- Document traversal strategy: BFS, DFS, Dijkstra-style routing, heuristic pathfinding, or policy-constrained routing.
- Review graph freshness, update logs, failures, closures, and dynamic conditions.
- Assess distributional effects when routes shift burdens across people, places, systems, or institutions.
- Disclose limitations, missing edges, stale data, and assumptions.
- Maintain appeal, review, and override mechanisms for consequential routing decisions.
