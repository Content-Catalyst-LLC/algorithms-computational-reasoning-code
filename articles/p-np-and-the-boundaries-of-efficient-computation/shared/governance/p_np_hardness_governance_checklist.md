# P, NP, and Hardness Governance Checklist

- Define whether the problem is decision, search, optimization, counting, or verification.
- Define input-size dimensions and relevant parameters.
- State the claimed class: P, NP, NP-hard, NP-complete, or unknown.
- Identify certificates for yes instances when claiming membership in NP.
- Define the verifier and its cost.
- Document reduction evidence for hardness claims.
- Separate exact, approximate, heuristic, randomized, bounded, and timed-out claims.
- Benchmark practical behavior and preserve timeout records.
- Preserve certificates, witnesses, solver status, optimality gaps, and audit logs where possible.
- Communicate computational limits in plain language.
