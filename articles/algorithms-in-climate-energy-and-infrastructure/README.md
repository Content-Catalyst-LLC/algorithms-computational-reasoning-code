# Algorithms in Climate, Energy, and Infrastructure

This companion folder supports the Sustainable Catalyst article **Algorithms in Climate, Energy, and Infrastructure**.

It provides reproducible, teaching-oriented workflows for climate-risk modeling, energy demand forecasting, grid dispatch, renewable integration, environmental monitoring, predictive maintenance, infrastructure asset management, digital twins, resilience planning, transportation and water systems, decarbonization pathways, environmental justice, uncertainty, validation, audit trails, and responsible infrastructure governance.

## Article sequence

- Previous: `algorithms-in-health-care-and-public-health`
- Current: `algorithms-in-climate-energy-and-infrastructure`
- Next: `algorithms-in-education-and-learning-systems`

## What this folder demonstrates

- how climate, energy, and infrastructure algorithm systems can be reviewed for public impact, climate exposure, reliability impact, equity readiness, validation readiness, monitoring readiness, governance readiness, and maintenance readiness;
- how infrastructure risk, grid balance, maintenance priority, avoided loss, resilience risk, and governance readiness can be calculated in small examples;
- how high-impact public systems can require public-value and equity review;
- how high-reliability systems can require reliability and safety review;
- how climate-exposed systems can require climate-equity review;
- how infrastructure algorithm governance requires scenario review, uncertainty communication, validation, stress testing, sensor coverage review, human override, emergency protocols, monitoring, audit trails, and stop rules.

## Repository structure

- `python/` — reference climate, energy, and infrastructure risk audit workflow and tests
- `r/` — diagnostic summaries and figures
- `sql/` — schema and queries for infrastructure systems, resilience risk scores, and governance controls
- `calculators/` — self-contained infrastructure-risk, grid-balance, maintenance-priority, avoided-loss, governance-readiness, and resilience-risk calculators
- `canvas/` — Canvas-ready cards, schemas, and exports
- `docs/` — method notes, data dictionary, scenario review, environmental justice review, validation notes, governance review, and use boundaries
- language folders — compact examples across Python, R, Julia, SQL, Haskell, Rust, Go, C, C++, Fortran, Java, TypeScript, Prolog, and Racket

All datasets and examples are synthetic and educational. They are not engineering advice, climate-risk certification, energy-market advice, utility operations advice, emergency-management advice, legal advice, regulatory compliance advice, or operational guidance.
