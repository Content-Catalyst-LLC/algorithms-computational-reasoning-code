# Method Notes

This folder treats climate, energy, and infrastructure algorithms as public-risk governance systems.

The core method is:

1. Define system purpose, physical boundaries, decision role, affected people, and public authority.
2. Map data sources, sensors, assets, hazards, dependencies, coverage gaps, and missingness.
3. Document assumptions, time horizon, spatial scale, scenarios, uncertainty, and exclusions.
4. Assess safety, reliability, climate exposure, equity, public value, and environmental impact.
5. Validate using historical tests, stress scenarios, sensitivity analysis, expert review, and physical plausibility.
6. Define human override, emergency procedures, public review, contestability, and accountability.
7. Monitor drift, sensor failure, incidents, maintenance, inequity, service quality, and model aging.
8. Pause, limit, roll back, or retire systems when unsafe, misleading, inequitable, or outdated.

The examples are dependency-light and educational.
